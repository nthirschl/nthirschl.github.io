About CV

I provide quantitative research and analytic support for organizations working on social problems: nonprofits, public agencies, academic researchers, and mission-driven businesses. Trained as a quantitative sociologist, I have a decade of experience analyzing administrative data, surveys, and public datasets across food systems, education, labor markets, and access to services.

I most enjoy working as an ongoing research partner rather than a one-off consultant, collaborating on data strategy, research design, as well as handling the nuts-and-bolts stuff of analysis and interpretation.

Currently, my work focuses on food insecurity and food access measurement in the Vermont, in partnership with the Vermont Foodbank.

Services

  • Survey design and analysis
  • Literature review and evidence synthesis
  • Program evaluation and impact analysis
  • Statistical modeling and machine learning
  • Demographic analysis and projections
  • Mapping and geospatial analysis
  • Data cleaning, integration, and quality assessment
  • Data visualization and reporting
  • Measurement and metrics development
